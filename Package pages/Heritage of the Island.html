About

Sri Lanka, an island nation off the southern coast of India, boasts a rich and diverse heritage that spans thousands of years. Its cultural, historical, and natural heritage sites attract visitors from around the globe. From ancient ruins to colonial architecture, serene tea plantations to vibrant festivals, Sri Lanka's heritage is a tapestry woven with layers of tradition and history.

Anuradhapura

An ancient city steeped in history, served as the capital of Sri Lanka for over a millennium. Its sprawling ruins, including majestic stupas and monastic complexes, offer a glimpse into the island's rich past.

Polonnaruwa

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, flourished as the island's capital in the 11th century. Under the rule of King Parakramabahu I, the city witnessed a golden age of art and architecture, leaving behind impressive monuments like the Gal Vihara with its magnificent rock-cut Buddha statues.

Kandy

Nestled amidst misty hills, was the last capital of the Sinhalese kings. Home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic, it remains a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists. The city's annual Esala Perahera is a dazzling display of cultural heritage, drawing visitors from far and wide.

Nuwara Eliya

Known as "Little England," exudes colonial charm with its cool climate and tea plantations. Established by British planters in the 19th century, it offers visitors the chance to explore lush landscapes, tour tea factories, and unwind amidst picturesque gardens reminiscent of a bygone era.
